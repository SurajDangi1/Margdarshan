# Regarding the Programme

Sant Nirankari Mandal offers scholarships under the Baba Guru Bachan Singh Scholarship Scheme 2023.
The Baba Guru Bachan Singh Scholarship Scheme 2021 is aimed at financially strapped individuals who want to enrol in certificate and degree programmes.
Supporting in this manner will lower the after-twelfth dropout rate.
The educational division will choose applicants based on their needs and provide them with financial aid for courses like B.A. and B.A. (HONS), B.Com and B.Com (Hons), B.Sc, Diploma in Engineering, Hotel Management, MCA, Diploma/Degree or Sports, Instrumental Music, and Vocal Music Training from a recognised institute/college in India.

# Rewards

In accordance with this programme, students will receive either a stipend of INR 50,000 per year or a tuition waiver, whichever is more practical. Students in the 10th or 12th grades who get 60% or higher will receive a 100% scholarship, and those who achieve 50% to 59% will receive 50% financial aid in the form of tuition support.

# How to apply ?

Step 1: Select the 'Apply Now' option from the list below.
Step 2: Carefully read the instructions and complete the application form.
Step 3: Enclose the affidavit and other necessary paperwork.
Step 4: Send the completed application form and any required documentation to the following address through express mail.

# Eligibility

To be eligible for the Baba Gurbachan Singh Scholarship Scheme 2023, you must meet the following requirements:

- You must be a first-year regular student enrolled in an accredited Indian university's diploma or degree programme.
- The annual household income should not be higher than Rs. 3.50 lakh.
- must have received at least 60% on the 12th grade exam.
- should not have failed their previous qualifying test or been detained by the institute for insubordination or a comparable offence.
- must not be receiving any other or comparable scholarships.
- Neither management quotas nor any other direct quotas may have been used for admittance.

# Documents

- The institution/university provides the most recent version of the fee receipt.
- Class 10 and 12 mark sheets, as well as the most recent paystubs and IT returns for any family members who are employed, or a copy of the SDM-issued family income certificate.
- a duplicate of the passbook or cheque.
- Ration card, Aadhaar card, voter ID, Pan card, or any other type of documentation, in Xerox form.
- You must provide proof of all expenses, including those that the institution's principle has approved, such as tuition, housing costs, books, etc.
- a document from the institution attesting that the household income of the student is not more than $3.50 lacs, that the student did not apply for admission using the Management/Convener quota or any other quota, and that the student has not received any other financial aid.
