# about this Scholarship
The Ministry of Minority Affairs (MOMA) offers Post Matric Scholarships for Minorities in order to help students from minority communities. Muslims, Sikhs, Christians, Buddhists, Zoroastrians (Paris), and Jains have been designated as minority communities by the Government of India. Students from class 11 to PhD are eligible for this post-matric scholarship for minorities, which provides up to INR 10,000 each year to continue higher education.


# Documents

An candidate must upload a certain set of papers on the application portal while applying for the Post-Matric Scholarship for Minorities. The following documents must be provided by a candidate:

 - Certificate of domicile for the relevant state
 - Student self-declaration of minority community certificate Self-attested copy of prior academic mark sheet
 - A copy of the Aadhar Enrollment/Aadhar Card Income Certificate issued by the specified state/UT administration is required.
 - Proof of a bank account in the student's name or a joint account with mother/father



# How can you apply?
Those who want to apply for the Post-Matric Scholarship for Minorities can do so through the National Scholarship Portal, often known as the NSP. The National Scholarship Programme (NSP) is a government site for different officially supported scholarship programmes. It offers a one-stop shop for all scholarship-related issues, including scholarship listing, application, receipt, sanction, and disbursement. It makes the process easier for students because they no longer have to rely on several sources for any scholarship-related information.


To apply for the Post-Matric Scholarship for Minorities through NSP, please follow the steps below:

 - The applicant must first go to the NSP's official website.
 - Then, fill out the registration form to become a new user.
 - Enter your address, name, date of birth, mobile number, gender, and email address, and then choose a scholarship to apply for.
 - When this step is completed, an OTP will be generated. Use this OTP to validate the cellphone number.
 - Log in to NSP and fill out the form using the OTP received on the registered cell phone.
 - This will produce an application ID and password for future usage.

# Eligibility Criteria

This programme is intended for brilliant pupils from low-income households. Applicants must ensure that they are above the 80th percentile of successful students in the relevant stream in the class 12 board exam when applying for this merit-based award. More information regarding the scholarship's qualifying requirements may be found below.


Applicants must be in the top 20% of their respective boards' class 12 merit lists (SEB/CBSE/ICSE).
Candidates must be enrolled in normal programmes of graduation/postgraduate/professional studies in colleges/universities approved by AICTE, UGC, MCI, DCI, or other regulatory authorities.
Candidates from the reserved categories of SC, ST, OBC, and PWD will have a 15%, 7.5%, 27%, and 5% quota, respectively.
