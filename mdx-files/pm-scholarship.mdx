# Regarding the Programme

The Welfare and Rehabilitation Board, Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India has created the Prime Minister's Scholarship Scheme for Central Armed Police Forces and Assam Rifles 2022â€“2023 for the dependant widows 
and wards of Central Armed Police Forces and Assam Rifles members. This programme has also been expanded to include the dependant children of State Police employees who die in terrorist or Naxal assaults. Under this programme,
the chosen individuals would receive up to INR 3,000 each month.


# How to apply ?

The following methods can be used to apply for the scholarship by qualified candidates:
Step 1: select the 'Apply Now' option shown below. 
Step 2: Select 'New Registration' under 'Applicant Corner' from the menu.
Step 3: At this point, select "Click here for other Scholarship Schemes hosted on NSP for AY 2022-23."
Step 4: Carefully read the instructions, choose the assignment, and press "Continue." 
Step 5: Determine the state of residence, the scholarship category (Pre Matric), the kind of programme (scholarship programme), the applicant's gender, and enter their name, date of birth, mobile number, and email address.
Provide the bank information (Bank name, IFSC Code, Account Number) in step six.
Step 7: Choose Aadhaar or a bank account number as your method of identification and press the "Register" button.
Step 8: An OTP will be created when the cellphone number has been validated.
Step 9: Enter your login information and complete the application form.
Step 10: An application ID and password will be generated upon the successful submission of the application form and may be used in the future.


# Eligibility

An applicant must meet one of the following requirements in order to be considered:

- be the child or widow of a CAPF or AR member who died while on duty, while serving in an election, or who was incapacitated as a result of their employment with the government, or who is an ex-member of the CAPF or AR who has received a gallantry award, OR
- be a dependent or widow of an active-duty or former CAPF or AR employee, or
- be a dependant ward of State Police Officers killed in terrorist or Naxal assaults.
- be enrolled in a first professional degree programme in the fields of engineering, medicine, dentistry, veterinary medicine, BBA, BCA, B. Pharma, B.Sc. (nursing, agriculture, etc.), MBA, and MCA, among others, at a facility approved by government regulatory bodies like the University Grant Commission (UGC), the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE), and the Medical Council of India (MCI).
- have earned at least 60% in their Minimum Entry Qualification (MEQ), which must be a Class 12 diploma, graduate degree, or something comparable (For new applicants). 
- a Class 12 diploma, graduate degree, or an equivalent (for new candidates) must have obtained at least 60% of their Minimum Entry Qualification (MEQ). 

Note: Candidates who were unable to submit their renewal applications by the deadline will be allowed a grace year to do so.

# Rewards
INR 3,000 per month for girls
INR 2,500 per month for boys


# Documents

- The H.O.O. will issue a Service Certificate.
- Class 12/Diploma/Graduation or equivalent marksheet with the accompanying certificate, duly attested.
            - PPO and Discharge Certificate and Book (Required for categories A through F)
            - Certificate of Disabilities (Required for Categories B and D)
            - Certificate of Death (Required for categories A and C)
            - Award of the Certificate of Gallantry (Required for category E)
- a state government certificate (required) stating that state police officers were murdered during terrorist or Naxal violence
